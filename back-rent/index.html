What you can collect
$3,500 + two months future rent

Through the City's Eviction Diversion Program, a landlord can elect the Targeted Financial Assistance track — the City pays the tenant's past-due rent up to a $3,500 recovery cap, plus two months of future rent. It's an assistance payment, not a loan, paid by direct deposit.

  • Owed more than $3,500? You still qualify — you can apply and settle the balance at the $3,500 cap, plus the two months of future rent.
  • No application fee for the program — it's not a loan and there's no repayment.
  • We only get paid if you do — 33% of the funds the City pays, success-based.
